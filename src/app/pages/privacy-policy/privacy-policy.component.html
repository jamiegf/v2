i-POOLs’ Privacy and Personal Data Policy

This Privacy Policy forms part of i-pools’ Terms & Conditions and all definitions are the same as set out in that document.

Summary

At i-pools we are committed to preserving and safeguarding the privacy of our Users and the security of their Personal Data (personally identifiable information). Personal Data includes any information which can identify an individual such as your name or your address and any information which is stored alongside this identifying data. We seek to ensure that your Personal Data is never used for an inappropriate purpose and that it is safeguarded to every extent possible.

The collection, storing and processing of Personal Data is regulated by the European General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) which become effective on 25th May 2018 and our policies are fully compliant with this legislation.

Our Privacy Policy explains what Personal Data we collect and why, what happens to any personal data that you provide to us, or that we collect from you whilst you visit the Website; the basis on which we collect, store and process your data; and your rights under GDPR.

i-pools is registered with the Information Commissioner’s Office: licence number: ZA024208.

We update this Policy from time to time so please do review it regularly. This policy was last updated on 25 May 2018.

■ data that you provide when you communicate with us by any means.

The Personal Data that we ask you to provide (detailed below) is collected as processed by Us for the following purposes:

■ we are legally required to verify each User’s age and address before providing the Services; or

■ we have a “legitimate interest” in providing you with the Services; or

■ we would like to communicate with you to remind you of upcoming events or special offer.

We use the services of a third party service provider to carry out the verification process and they may, in order to provide this service, transfer your Personal Data outside the European Economic Area (“EEA”) (see below). We may also, if you provide Us with consent, process your Personal data for the purpose of communicating with you regarding our Services, such as reminders of upcoming events or special offers. You may, at any stage, withdraw your Consent to receive such communications.

We use the Personal Data that we collect from you to provide our Services to you and to constantly improve our Services. In addition to this we may use data for one or more of the following purposes:

We will NEVER disclose your Personal Data to any other party other than in accordance with our Terms & Conditions and this Privacy Policy or in the circumstances detailed below:

We store all Personal Data in secure databases in encrypted form and we do our utmost to ensure that all reasonable steps are taken to make sure that your data is treated stored securely. Unfortunately the sending of information via the internet is not totally secure and on occasion such information can be intercepted. We cannot guarantee the absolute security of data that you send us electronically, so sending such information is done so entirely at your own risk.

Transferring Data Outside the EU

In operating our Website it may become necessary to transfer data that we collect from you to locations outside of the European Union or the EEA for processing. By providing your Personal Data to us, you implicitly agree to this transfer and/or processing all of which will continue to be done in accordance with our Privacy Policy.

We will only transfer your Personal Data outside of the EU/EEA where:

■ you have given your explicit consent, or

■ it is necessary for us to set up or fulfil a contract you have entered into with us; or

■ to comply with a legal duty or obligation.

If we do transfer your Personal Data outside of the EU/EEA, we will take measures to ensure it is protected to the same standards as it would be within the EU/EEA by relying on one of the following:

■ We will use contracts that require the recipient to protect your Personal Data to the same standards as it would be within the EU/EEA.

■ We will use contracts that require the recipient to protect your Personal Data to the same standards as it would be within the EU/EEA.

In some instances we may be compelled by law to disclose your Personal Data to a third party and may have limited control over how it is protected by that party.

Use of Cookies

Cookies provide information about the computer used by a User to access the Website. We may use cookies where appropriate to provide the Services or to gather information about your computer in order to assist us in running and improving our Website. This information will not identify you personally as it is purely statistical data. As a result, cookies are not considered to be part of your Personal Data.

Where used, these cookies are downloaded to your computer and temporarily stored on the computer’s hard drive.

You can adjust the settings on your computer to decline any cookies if you prefer. This can easily be done by activating the ‘reject cookies’ setting on your computer. However , this may result in you not being able to access the Services.

Third Party Links

On occasion we include links to third parties’ websites on the Website. Where we provide a link it does not mean that we endorse or approve that site’s policy towards visitor privacy and security. You should review their privacy policy before sending them any personal data.

Access to Personal Data

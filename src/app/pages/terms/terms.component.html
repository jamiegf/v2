5.5 Pool Settlement and Prizefund Payout

Pools will be settled as soon as reasonably practicable following the End Time of the pool (or such other time as is defined in the Pool Rules).

MiPools relies on data provided to us by Sports Radar and/or other third party data providers, to be used as the source data to determine pool results. If the data provider does not provide the relevant data to settle a pool, MiPoolsreserves the right to settle the pool based on alternative publically available information sources as it may, at its discretion, determine. If no alternative public information is available the relevant pool or question may be void. See paragraph 5.6 (Cancelled Events/Voiding Pools).

If there is an investigation or dispute, data provided to us by a third party provider, evidence that supports alternative public information and/or our transaction log will be provided as supporting evidence.

When a pool is settled the Net Prizefund is calculated (the total entry fees in the pool minus the Platform Fee) and paid out to the Users in the prize positions when all relevant results have been updated and finalised.

The payout structure of a pool is determined by the number of entrants in the pool and the table structure (ie Unlimited, 10 Max, Heads Up etc). The standard payout structure for unlimited pools will add more prizes the more entries are in the pool as detailed below. The payout structure is always stated in the pool description at the top of the pool, but the number of prizes awarded and the prizes themselves may change as more players enter.

Most standard pools follow a Top three payout structure which is as follows:

■ 1st place 70%, 2nd place 20%, 3rd place 10%

■ (with exception of the weekly football jackpot rollover, daily race and football matchrollover)

■ Each pool has the ability to use its own individual payout structure which is always displayed in the individual game 'How to play' page(s).

Fantasy Pools with Target Score (unless otherwise stated):

■ for Fantasy pools that require Users to reach a target score to win the pool 100% of the Net Prizefund will go to the winner(s).

Survivor Unlimited Pools (unless otherwise stated):

■ 100% of the Net Prizefund goes to the User(s) who last(s) longest.

Predictor And Fantasy 10 Max Pools:

■ for a full table of ten Users 66.7% of the net Prizefund will be paid to the Winner of each pool and 33.3% will be paid to the Runner-Up in each pool. If the pool contains between 2 and 9 Users 100% of the pool will go to the winner.

Survivor 10 Max Pools:

■ 100% of the Net Prizefund goes to the User(s) who last(s) longest.

Heads Up Pools (Predictor, Fantasy or Survivor):

■ 100% of the Net Prizefund will always be paid to the winner(s) of the pool.

Unless otherwise stated, if two or more Users tie for a position, the relevant Prizes are added together and divided by the number of tied Users to calculate the amount awarded to each User.

For example: if three Users tie for 2nd Prize, then 2nd, 3rd and 4th Prizes (if there are 3rd and 4th Prizes) will be added together and split equally between the three tying Users. The amount of the Prize will be added to the User’s balance immediately after the Pool has settled.

Prizes will be rounded to the nearest penny.

5.9 Rollover Pools

For some pools, designated as Rollover Pools in the pool title and header, the prizefund will only be paid out in the event that one or more players answer all the questions in the pool correctly and precisely.

There is generally only one prize in Rollover Pools which may be shared by more than one winning player. Some Rollover Pools may payout consolation prizes or dividends for "near misses". Details of these will be published in the Pool Rules.

If a Rollover Pool is not won, the prizefund, minus the platform Fee, will "rollover" to the next designated Rollover Pool. This process will continue until the prizefund is won.

If the result of a question in a Rollover Pool is void under the standard Pool Rules, so the Rollover Prizefund cannot be won and "near miss" dividends are not available, then any player(s) that answered all the other questions correctly and precisely will be reimbursed their entry fee(s).

If a Rollover Pool is void, under the standard Pool Rules, then any entry fees added to the existing pool will be returned, no platform Fee will be charged and the remaining prizefund will "rollover" to the next designated Rollover Pool.

15.2 Progressive Jackpot

MiPools is currently offering a Progressive Jackpot which gives all registered Users the opportunity to win a Prize offered by MiPools by answering all the questions correctly in certain designated pools. Progressive Jackpot pools will be designated by the Progressive Jackpot symbol (PJ).

The amount of the Progressive Jackpot Prizefund is published on the Website and updated regularly. If a player wins the Progressive Jackpot they 75% of the progressive jackpot balance will be paid out to the winner(s) (minus tax and administration fee) and 25% will be added to the next progressive Jackpot balance.

Only some Unlimited Predictor pools are designated “Progressive Jackpot” and labelled accordingly. To qualify to win the Progressive Jackpot a User must enter one of these pre-designated pools AND pay the Progressive Jackpot entry fee (currently £1 or 100 Points).

Having entered a Progressive Jackpot and paid the Progressive Jackpot entry fee, to win the Progressive Jackpot, a User must correctly and precisely predict ALL of the outcomes of ALL questions in the relevant pool. If any question in a Progressive Jackpot designated pool is void, the Progressive Jackpot cannot be won in that Pool and any Progressive Jackpot stakes will be refunded in full.

For example, for the question "How many goals will be scored in the match?", if 5 goals were scored, the user would have to select the answer "5 or (more)" to answer correctly and precisely. If they had selected "3 (or more)" or "4 (or more)", they would not have selected the precise answer.

For the purposes of winning the Progressive Jackpot, answers will be rounded to the nearest option which was available for users to select as an outcome. So if the possible answers for a quesion were provided in steps of 5, (e.g. 0, 5, 10, 15, 20 etc) and the outcome is not a multiple of 5, the outcome will be rounded to the nearest 5 for the purposes of winning the Progressive Jackpot.

The Progressive Jackpot prize will be equal to 75% of the balance at the time (minus Pools Betting Duty of 15% and Administration Fee of 10%). The balance of 25% will be paid into the next progressive jackpot.

In the event that two or more Users win the Progressive Jackpot in the same pool or on the same day, the Progressive Jackpot Prizefund will be shared between these Users.

To receive the Progressive Jackpot a User will be required to provide documents as evidence of their age, identity and address. Employees of MiPools are not eligible to win the Progressive Jackpot.

15.3 Jackpot bonus

Some pools, designated with a "£1M" symbol in the Pools Lobby, carry a £1 million jackpot bonus which can be won by any player that enters the pool and successfully meets the winning criteria for winning the Jackpot Bonus, which will always be stated in the Pool Description. Jackpot pools cannot be entered using points (see paragraph 6.2 above) and can only be entered with cash. The Jackpot Bonus cannot be won in Challenge pools and can only be won in public pools available to all players on the Website.

To win a Jackpot Bonus in a Predictor or Fantasy pool, the User must answer each question correctly and precisely and according to the Rules.

To win a Jackpot Bonus in a Survivor "streak" pool, the User must make the specified number of correct selections in a row. For Football Survivor Jackpot pools, this is 20 correct selections in a row; for a Golf Survivor Jackpot pool this would require 10 correct selections in a row.

Only one Jackpot Bonus can be won in any one pool; so if two (or more) players win the Jackpot Bonus in the same pool, which can be two different tables or stake sizes in the same pool (eg the £5 table AND the £25 table in the same Survivor pool), the Jackpot Bonus will be shared by these players.

Only Users with accounts that have been fully verified prior to entering can win the Jackpot Bonus and only entries that are made prior to the pool start time will qualify (ie with a time stamp before the Close date of the pool). Any User that meets the winning criteria for the Jackpot will be required to provide proof of age and address before receiving their Jackpot prize.

If any question in a Jackpot Pool is voided, the Jackpot bonus cannot be won.

Jackpot pools carry an extra fee which is added to the normal platform Fee for each Jackpot pool.

The Jackpot Bonus is arranged by a third party, Risq Capital Limited, and any winning Jackpot bonus will only be paid out after the winning entry and the winning player's details have been verified and agreed by them.

Employees of MiPools, or any associated company, are not eligible to win the Jackpot Bonus.
